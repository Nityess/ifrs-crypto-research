## Overview

This whitepaper provides a structured analysis of how **International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS)** currently governs the classification, measurement, and disclosure of crypto assets — including Bitcoin, Ethereum, stablecoins, NFTs, and DeFi instruments.

Crypto assets do not fit neatly within existing accounting categories. Practitioners must navigate a patchwork of standards — **IAS 2, IAS 38, IFRS 9, and IFRS 13** — often with no dedicated guidance. This paper cuts through that complexity with practical clarity.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Default Classification:** Most crypto assets are classified as **Intangible Assets under IAS 38** — not cash, not financial instruments
- **Two Measurement Models:** Cost model (with impairment asymmetry) vs. Revaluation model (only if an active market exists under IFRS 13)
- **Critical Asymmetry:** Under the cost model, impairment losses are recognised when prices fall — but price recoveries **cannot** be recognised until disposal
- **IFRS vs. US GAAP Gap:** FASB's ASU 2023-08 now requires fair value measurement for crypto assets. IFRS has no equivalent dedicated standard — yet
- **Emerging Challenges:** Stablecoins, NFTs, and DeFi instruments fall outside even the IFRIC 2019 guidance, requiring significant professional judgment
- **Nepal Perspective:** Implications for ICAN-qualified CAs operating in a jurisdiction where NRB has issued AI/digital asset guidelines but crypto-specific accounting rules remain nascent
